History of New Wave City

In the summer of 1992, NWC co-founders Skip and Shindog found themselves in a band that was breaking up. Skip, a regular on the San Francisco club scene from the days of the I-Beam, Earl's, Club Anon, Echo Beach, 181 Club and others, was disillusioned with the changes that had taken place in music and attitude. Raves and house music had taken over the scene, and everyone working in the clubs - bouncers, cashiers, bartenders, and even the coat check person - was suddenly rude. Nightclubs had become meat markets.

Skip was fed up and felt that he could do a better job. Thus inspired, he decided to try putting together a "reunion" party of sorts for friends, DJs, and employees from the Haight Street nightclub called The I-Beam, and showcase the music of the early ‚Äò80s we all still loved. A partnership with now ex-bandmate Shindog, an experienced DJ and promoter, was formed.

The goal of the intrepid duo was to try to bring back the camaraderie, look, and feeling of those bygone nightlife days. With all of that in mind, they produced their first event together. Held on a Tuesday night in August at The I-Beam (because Tuesday nights were huge in the early ‚Äò80s), the event featured an ‚Äô80s fashion show organized and curated by third partner, Samantha Hartley. Skip and special guest DJ Brian Raffi (former resident DJ at The I-Beam) split the turntable duties, and the party was a raging success. Everyone kept saying, "Please do it again!" And...

Somehow, someway, since that groundbreaking night in 1992, **New Wave City is now the longest running club event in San Francisco!**

WHAT IS NEW WAVE CITY ALL ABOUT?

New Wave City (NWC) is the ‚ÄúFirst & Foremost ‚Äò80s Dance Party,‚Äù originating in **1992**, and currently presented once a month on the third Saturday at the Cat Club in San Francisco. NWC is devoted to playing the best in alternative dance music from the New Wave era of the late ‚Äò70s and early ‚Äò80s. The promoters, hosts, and DJs of NWC are Skip and Shindog.

At any NWC event, you can expect a cool crowd of knowledgeable New Wave music fans from various ages and walks of life, who come together to dance, socialize and celebrate the music of their lives. Each event features a tribute to one of the seminal acts of the period, such as **Depeche Mode, The Smiths, The Cure, New Order, Siouxsie and the Banshees**, etc. You'll hear songs and music videos from the featured artist throughout the evening as well as the rest of the best of New Wave.

Special events such as **New Wave Prom, Winter Ball, The Black & Black Ball, or NWC Anniversary Party** often have featured added attractions like: special guest DJs (Terri Nunn of Berlin, Kurt Harland from Information Society), live performances (Debora Iyall of Romeo Void), fashion shows, dance or fashion contests, performance pieces, go-go dancers, and much more.
